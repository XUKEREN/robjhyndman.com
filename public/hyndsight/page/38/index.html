Why I don't like statistical tests

24 August 2009

forecasting, statistics

It may come as a shock to discover that a statistician does not like statistical tests. Isn’t that what statistics is all about? Unfortunately, in some disciplines statistical analysis does seem to consist almost entirely of hypothesis testing, and therein lies the problem. The standard practice is to construct a hypothesis test to determine if some attribute of the data is “significant” or not, with the standard p-value threshold of 5%.

Maintaining local LaTeX files

14 August 2009

computing, LaTeX

If you use LaTeX, then you probably have a bib file — a data base of all the papers and books that you have cited. It is much more efficient to keep one database in one location, than have multiple copies of it floating around your hard drive. (Or even worse, have different bib files created for different papers.) You might also have a few of your own style files, and again it is best to keep these in a central location and not have duplicates all over the place.
